Sapphire

Here are more about Sapphire

Sapphire is a precious gemstone that is a variety of corundum, an aluminum oxide mineral. Sapphires are typically blue, but can also be found in other colors.

Color: Blue: The most valued sapphires are a medium-deep cornflower blue. Fancy sapphires: Sapphires that are yellow, orange, pink, purple, green, violet, or intermediate hues. Color-changing sapphires: Sapphires that change color depending on the lighting. For example, they may be blue in daylight and purple under incandescent light. Parti sapphires: Sapphires that have two or more colors.

Composition: Chemical composition Sapphires are made of aluminum oxide (\(Al_{2}O_{3}\)), which is two parts aluminum to three parts oxygen. Trace elements Sapphires contain small amounts of iron, titanium, cobalt, lead, chromium, vanadium, magnesium, boron, and silicon.

Uses: Jewelry: Sapphires are a popular choice for jewelry. Windows: Sapphire crystals are used in high durability windows. Wristwatches: Sapphire crystals are used in wristwatch crystals and movement bearings. Electronics: Sapphires can be used in electronics.

History: The name sapphire comes from the Greek word sappheiros, which means "precious blue gem". Sapphires have been mined and valued since at least 800 BC. Sapphires were used in the High Priest's breastplate in the First Temple in 957 BCE.
