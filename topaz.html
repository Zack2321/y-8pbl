Topaz

Here are more about Topaz

Topaz is a durable gemstone that is used in jewelry and other adornments. It is a silicate mineral made of aluminum and fluorine.

Physical properties: Topaz is a very hard mineral, measuring 8 on the Mohs scale. It is a pleochroic gemstone, meaning that it can appear different colors from different angles. Topaz is colorless in its natural state, but impurities can give it other colors. Topaz can be treated with heat or radiation to change its color.

Uses: Topaz is used in jewelry and other adornments. Topaz is also used as a raw material in the production of refractory materials, fluorine compounds, ceramic, and glass.

Colors: Blue topaz is the most common color, but topaz can also be pink, red, orange, yellow, and brown. Imperial topaz is a vibrant orange with pink undertones and is the most prized color. Pink topaz can come from Pakistan, Brazil, and Russia.

History: Topaz is mentioned in the Bible as one of the "fiery stones" that Moses received on the mountain of God. Topaz is also mentioned in the Revelation of John as one of the twelve gemstones that adorn the foundations of the walls of the Heavenly Jerusalem.

Special about Topaz: Topaz has long been associated with soothing, healing and re-motivating, and has been called the gemstone of good fortune and love. It is also thought to give one the energy to promote forgiveness and truthfulness. Wearing Topaz has been said to help stabilise one's emotions and calm one's nerves.

Additional Information: Topaz is often found in igneous rocks such as granite and rhyolite. It is also found in alluvial deposits. The largest topaz ever found was a transparent, colorless stone weighing 596 pounds. Topaz is the birthstone for November and is associated with the zodiac sign Sagittarius.
